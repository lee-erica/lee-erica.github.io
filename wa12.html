Introduction

For Weekly Assignment 12, I chose to use the "wheat.json" file from the existing Vega datasets. According to the documentation, this source comes from William Playfair, a Scottish engineer who is often credited as the founder of statistical graphics, who plotted 250 years of wheat prices alongside weekly wages and the reigning monarch.

Interpretation of Visualizations

Based on my visualizations, it is clear that William Playfair was correct in that wheat was never cheap, because of the upwards trend in labor as shown by the data in wages. As wheat prices went up, so did the wages of those who were involved in mechanial labor to produce the wheat. This was an interesting set of data to understand how labor and profits go hand in hand.
